Camera: Canon Rebel T7

Big news! I got a new lens! I found a super good deal on a used Canon 70-300 4-5.6 IS USM lens! I know what you're thinking, what the hell does all that mean? Well lets break it down!

The 70-300 shows the field of view of the lens, also known as the zoom. So this lens can have a field of view from 70mm all the way up to 300mm!

Next up is the 4-5.6 part. This shows the focal length minimum of the lens at certain field of views. So this lens at 70mm, the minimum focal length is f/4. Then at 300mm, the minimum focal length is f/5.6.

Lastly, we have IS and USM. Those are just some acronyms for the capabilities of the camera. IS stands for image stabilization which helps shoot at a lower shutterspeed. USM stands for Ultrasonic Motor which provides better autofocus. These silly letters are unique to Canon lenses, different manufacturers will have different letters to explains these same capabilities.
